## Post 1
Date: June 18, 2026
Engagement: 41 comments, 2 reposts

Jay Acunzo has talked for a long time about 
"resonance over reach." And I agree with him.

Don't just chase impressions. Don't optimize every idea 
for the biggest possible audience. Don't water down your 
work until it's broadly legible but mostly forgettable.

Reach without resonance is just noise.

But for marketers, we don't get to opt out of reach.

I think what we really need is sequencing — resonance 
BEFORE reach. Make the thing specific enough to matter, 
spread, and be remembered. Our work still has to travel, 
but it travels further when it actually means something 
to someone first.

Key Insight: Resonance before reach — make content 
specific enough to matter first, then let it spread.
This is the antidote to generic AI-produced content.

## Post 2
Date: June 17, 2026
Engagement: 63 comments, 2 reposts

"How would you start a marketing strategy from scratch?"

The annoying but true answer is: it depends. But because 
that's less fun, I would start with one question:

How is this business supposed to grow?

Is the intended growth mechanism raw discovery?
→ SEO, comparison pages, and educational content matter most.

Is it product-led growth?
→ Onboarding content, use-case content, templates, 
  lifecycle emails, and activation loops matter more.

Is it sales-led?
→ First priority might be the sales asset the team has 
  been begging for — a buyer guide or follow-up email sequence.

After this, look for the shortest path to proving 
marketing can help:
- Discovery-led motion → fix the highest-intent pages
- Sales-led motion → create the asset sales needs this month
- Product-led motion → improve content that shortens time-to-value

Key Insight: Before building any content strategy, first
understand how the business actually grows — then make
that specific motion work better.

## Post 3
Date: June 15, 2026
Engagement: 48 comments, 1 repost

"What's our AI visibility like?"
If my CEO or CMO asked me that, here's what I'd do.

First, run our brand through HubSpot's AEO Grader. 
Then summarize what stands out.

I did this for SparkToro. The report correctly identified 
us as a niche audience research tool. And it picked up on 
one of our biggest advantages: credibility with marketing 
practitioners.

Other parts need a human read:
- The competitive set is messy
- Share of voice info requires nuance — SparkToro shows up 
  at 24% in ChatGPT and Perplexity, but just 12.5% in Gemini
- Growth areas were plausible but generic

Here's what I'd do next:
1/ Find what's accurate — what is AI already getting right?
2/ Find the misconceptions — where is AI putting us in 
   the wrong category?
3/ Turn the gaps into priorities — website copy, content, 
   PR, sales enablement, and category education.

Then run this analysis again in 1-2 months
