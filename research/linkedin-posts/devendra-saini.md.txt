I rebuilt my entire SEO + content workflow as 18 agents 
and 2 human gates. Here's what it does:

For the past 4 weeks, I've been building an entire AI 
powered suite with a class UI that team loves to use 
and it actually mimics my actual workflow.

Here's what it does:

1. Raw keywords → a real strategy
Drop in a keyword dump from Keyword Planner, Ahrefs or 
Semrush. It turns that mess into a fully optimised topical 
map, clustering by real SERP similarity, embeddings, and 
multiple LLM decision layers.

Out comes detailed page outlines, search intent, UI/UX 
direction, NLP entities, programmatic logic, and sharp 
recommendations.

2. Outline → publish-ready draft
Hand the outline to a human writer, or pass it to the 
content orchestrator: an entire content team working 
one article at a time.

It analyses the brief, checks intent-satisfaction, and 
writes in your brand's voice with hyper customisable skills.

Then it gets strict. Factual and quality gates evaluate 
the content with more than 20 benchmarks. Only what passes 
reaches the editor, with detailed notes for review.

Under the hood:
→ Claude, OpenAI & Gemini + 4 specialised APIs
→ Multiple hyper-customisable RAG layers
→ Token caching built into the feedback loops

This isn't "AI writes your blog." It's an operating system 
for how a great SEO and content team actually thinks.

The teams that win the next few years won't be the ones 
replaced by agents. They'll be the ones who learned 
to direct them.

Claude code and Codex - take a bow!

Key Insight: The future of SEO content production is
semi-autonomous agent systems with human gates for quality
control — not full automation or full manual work.
