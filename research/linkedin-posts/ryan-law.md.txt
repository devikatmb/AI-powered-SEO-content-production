97% of llms.txt files got zero requests in May 🙃
No bots, no humans, nothing.

Here are 3 more findings from our research of 137K sites:

1. Slackbot fetched llms.txt files more often than 
   PerplexityBot did! Slack's link-preview bot beat 
   the AI search engine llms.txt was supposedly built for.

2. The biggest research crawler in the data identifies 
   as "prompt-injection-survey". Bad actors are already 
   probing llms.txt, because agents are built to trust it.

3. Zero AI bots go looking for llms.txt files that 
   don't exist.

Key Insight: llms.txt as a tactic is largely ineffective
right now — real data shows almost no AI bots are actually
using it, despite the hype around it.

Here's my human-centric vibe-writing process for going 
from keyword idea to published article in 91 minutes.

I've shared a lot of content about full-bore AI automation, 
using tons of chained skill files to build pipelines that 
create great content without any human intervention.

But... it's not a particularly fun process.

Both Tim Soulo and Mateusz Makosiewicz use AI to support 
their writing, but they do it very differently to me: 
they vibe-write, they talk and joke and ramble to their 
AI agent, and let the AI refine their creativity and 
impose structure.

So I tried out their process today and honestly, it was 
the most fun I've had writing for Ahrefs in ages.

All you need is a microphone, a head full of good ideas, 
and a capable marketing agent.

Key Insight: The best AI content workflow isn't full
automation — it's using AI to amplify human creativity
through vibe-writing, not replace it.
