## Post 1
Date: June 22, 2026
Engagement: 22 comments 1 repost

By the time a buyer books a demo with you, the decision 
is mostly made. And it wasn't made on your website.

The B2B buying journey stopped being a funnel a while ago. 
It looks more like a web. Before a prospect ever fills out 
a form, they have already:
→ Read a few G2 reviews
→ Lurked a Reddit thread comparing you to two competitors
→ Watched a YouTube teardown of your SaaS
→ Asked ChatGPT and Claude to both give a point of view 
  on who the best options are

Most of that happens in places your brand does not control, 
and it shapes the shortlist whether the buyer notices or not.

Here's what our team at Foundation Marketing has done 
for billion-dollar brands:
→ Stood up real Reddit presences
→ Optimized 150+ old YouTube videos
→ Used reviews to drive product change
→ Hired niche YouTubers to create BOFU content

The brands winning right now are the ones showing up 
in those rooms anyway.

Key Insight: Reddit is one of the most cited domains in 
AI Overviews and ChatGPT responses. Skip those conversations 
and AI models have no reason to mention you.

## Post 2
Date: June 18, 2026
Engagement: 28 comments

Your CEO isn't wrong to ask harder questions right now.
If impressions are up but pipeline isn't, something 
deeper is broken.

Here's the shift most teams are missing: up to 40% of 
buyer research is now happening inside AI tools like 
ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity. Buyers are making 
decisions before they ever visit your site.

Which leads to a tough question: when someone asks AI 
if your company is worth buying… Do you show up at all?

Most teams don't know. That's the gap.

Key Insight: AI visibility auditing is now a critical
first step for any B2B marketing team.
